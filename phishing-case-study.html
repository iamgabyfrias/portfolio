The Challenge

High-stakes security vulnerabilities in corporate environments

Corporate clients in finance and accounting sectors were consistently falling victim to phishing attacks, particularly those targeting their industry or appearing to come from internal "HR" departments. Traditional security awareness training wasn't translating to real-world behavior change.

Specific vulnerabilities included:

  • Employees downloading malware-riddled applications
  • Clicking attachments from compromised contacts
  • Falling for industry-specific phishing campaigns
  • Responding to fake HR communications

Design Process

A systematic approach to behavior change through experiential learning

1

Baseline Assessment

Conducted initial phishing simulations using KnowBe4 to establish current vulnerability levels across different client organizations.

2

Scenario Development

Created industry-specific phishing scenarios that reflected real-world attacks employees were likely to encounter.

3

Interactive Training Design

Developed live virtual training sessions via Zoom using interactive presentations that encouraged active participation rather than passive consumption.

4

Continuous Monitoring

Implemented monthly phishing simulations to track behavior change and identify areas needing additional reinforcement.

Results & Impact

Measurable behavior change and sustained improvement

Primary Achievement: Reduced client phish-prone percentage from 40.8% to 3.4% - a 92% improvement in security behavior.

"I'm now more conscious about what is emailed to me and have emailed you to make sure emails weren't scams."

— Corporate Training Participant

Sustained Impact: Employees began proactively reaching out to verify suspicious communications, demonstrating internalized security awareness rather than just compliance.

Key Success Factors:

  • Interactive delivery format that encouraged active participation
  • Industry-specific scenarios that felt relevant and realistic
  • Systematic reinforcement through monthly simulations
  • Focus on understanding rather than memorization

Lessons Learned

Insights for future security awareness programs

Real-World Relevance Matters

Training scenarios must closely mirror actual threats employees face in their specific industry and role.

Interactive > Passive

Live, interactive sessions with immediate feedback were far more effective than traditional video-based training.

Behavior Change Takes Time

Sustained improvement required 5-6 months of consistent reinforcement and measurement.

Trust Enables Learning

Creating a safe environment where employees could ask questions without judgment was crucial for engagement.
