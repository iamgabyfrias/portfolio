Interactive eLearning Module | Adult Professional Development

Challenge

Corporate clients in finance and accounting were consistently failing phishing simulations, particularly those targeting their industry or appearing to come from "HR." Employees were also downloading malware-riddled apps and clicking attachments from compromised contacts.

Solution

Designed an interactive scenario-based eLearning experience featuring realistic audio examples, decision-making branches, and immediate feedback. Used storytelling to create emotional connection while building practical recognition skills.

Impact

Reduced client phish-prone behavior from 40.8% to 3.4% through systematic monthly simulations and targeted follow-up training. Demonstrated measurable behavior change in high-stakes corporate environments.

Global Human Rights Education
International ELA Curriculum | Cross-Cultural Learning Design
16 Teachers Coached
2 Grade Levels
Challenge

How do you help students understand global human rights issues while making complex historical and contemporary injustices accessible and meaningful? Students needed to connect with experiences far from their own reality.

Solution

Created comprehensive curriculum exploring children's rights globally through multiple perspectives - Darfur refugees, Holocaust victims, Khmer Rouge survivors, and U.S. segregation. Used station-based learning with documentaries, music, literature, and local cultural parallels to help students process difficult topics with empathy and understanding.

Impact

Successfully helped students develop global awareness and empathy while building critical thinking skills about justice and human rights. Created framework for teaching sensitive topics across cultures that was adopted throughout the school's humanities curriculum.
